Your Guide to the NQF with EduEasy

The National Qualifications Framework (NQF) organizes South Africa’s qualifications into 10 levels. It helps you see what your education can do for you — from getting a job to advancing studies. Let’s break it down!

What Is the NQF?

The NQF is a system by SAQA that groups all qualifications (from school to PhD) into levels. It ensures your skills are recognized and helps with career planning.

Key Fact: NQF makes it easier to move between jobs, TVET, and university.

NQF Levels

From basic to advanced:

NQF LevelQualificationExample
1–4 High school education Grade 9 (Level 1), Matric (Level 4)
5–6 TVET certificates/diplomas National Certificate (Vocational)
7–8 University degrees Bachelor’s (Level 7), Honours (Level 8)
9–10 Advanced degrees Master’s (Level 9), PhD (Level 10)

Why Does the NQF Matter?

  • Plan Studies: See pathways (e.g., NQF 4 Matric to NQF 7 Bachelor’s).
  • Find Jobs: Employers use NQF to match skills.
  • Continue Learning: Universities/TVET require specific levels.

Examples for You

  • Got Matric?: NQF 4 — ready for TVET or university entry programs.
  • Want to Teach?: Aim for Bachelor of Education (NQF 7).
  • Work Experience?: Use RPL to gain NQF credits.
