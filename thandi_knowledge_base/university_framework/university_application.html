CAO vs. Direct Applications

EduEasy guides you through both options:

  • CAO (Central Applications Office): Apply to multiple KwaZulu-Natal universities (e.g., UKZN, DUT, MUT) with one form for approximately R250. Covers up to 6 programme choices.
  • Direct Applications: Apply to universities like UCT, Wits, or Stellenbosch via their own online portals. Fees vary by institution (R100-R300). EduEasy tracks all your applications for free.
Pro Tip: You can apply via both methods simultaneously. For example, use CAO for KZN universities and apply directly to UCT and Wits.

Admission Point Score (APS)

Your APS uses Matric results to check eligibility for university programmes. Each subject mark converts to points as follows:

Mark Range (%) APS Points Symbol
80–100% 7 Outstanding (7)
70–79% 6 Meritorious (6)
60–69% 5 Substantial (5)
50–59% 4 Moderate (4)
40–49% 3 Adequate (3)
30–39% 2 Elementary (2)
0–29% 1 Not Achieved (1)
Example Calculation: Medicine at UCT requires an APS of 38+ (out of 42 possible) with Mathematics and Physical Science at 70%+. Life Orientation is excluded from most APS calculations.

Tips for Success

  • Start Early: Apply with Grade 11 results (provisional) to beat deadlines and secure early conditional offers. Final Matric results are submitted later.
  • Check APS Requirements: Use EduEasy's free APS calculator to match your score to programme requirements. Don't waste application fees on programmes you don't qualify for.
  • Apply to Multiple Programmes: Choose 6 programmes in order of preference. Include "safety" choices (lower APS requirements) alongside your dream programmes.
  • Secure Funding First: Apply for NSFAS BEFORE or simultaneously with university applications. NSFAS opens in September 2025 and closes in November 2025. Thandi provides step-by-step NSFAS guidance.
  • Check Subject Requirements: Some programmes require specific Matric subjects. For example:
    • Engineering: Mathematics and Physical Science (both 60%+)
    • Medicine: Mathematics and Physical Science (both 70%+)
    • Law: No specific subject requirements, but 70%+ average recommended
    • Teaching: Depends on specialization
  • Keep Proof of Everything: Save copies of application confirmations, payment receipts, and correspondence with universities.
