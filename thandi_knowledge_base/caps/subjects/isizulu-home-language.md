## Overview
CAPS isiZulu Home Language develops advanced proficiency in South Africa's most widely spoken indigenous language, covering literature, creative writing, language structures, and cultural communication. Essential for careers requiring isiZulu proficiency and cultural competency.

## CAPS Requirements
Structured progression: Grade 10 foundations (language structures, basic literature), Grade 11 advanced concepts (poetry, prose, drama, advanced grammar), Grade 12 integration and applications (literary analysis, creative writing, oral communication). Assessment: 25% SBA (oral presentations, creative writing, research projects), 75% final examination (Paper 1: Language Structures and Conventions, Paper 2: Literature, Paper 3: Writing and Presenting).

## Career Pathways
isiZulu Home Language opens pathways to:
- Teaching (isiZulu and African languages)
- Translation and interpretation services
- Journalism and media (isiZulu publications, radio, TV)
- Publishing and editing (isiZulu literature)
- Cultural heritage and museum work
- Government and public service (KwaZulu-Natal focus)
- Legal services (court interpretation, community law)
- Social work and community development
- Tourism and cultural guiding
- Arts and creative industries (writing, theater, film)

## University Requirements
Universities accept isiZulu Home Language for relevant programs:
- African Languages: Minimum 50-60% (Level 4-5)
- Education (Language Teaching): Minimum 50-60% (Level 4-5)
- Linguistics: Minimum 55-65% (Level 4-5)
- Social Sciences: Minimum 50-60% (Level 4-5)
- Journalism: Minimum 55-65% (Level 4-5)

## APS Calculations
isiZulu Home Language contributes fully to APS calculations. Particularly valuable for language, education, and social science programs. Provides cultural competency advantage for careers in KwaZulu-Natal and Gauteng.

## CAPS Cultural Advantage
CAPS isiZulu Home Language emphasizes cultural heritage, oral traditions, and contemporary isiZulu literature - providing pathways to careers serving isiZulu-speaking communities and preserving indigenous knowledge systems.
