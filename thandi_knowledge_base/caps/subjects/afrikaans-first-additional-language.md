## Overview
CAPS Afrikaans First Additional Language develops communication skills in Afrikaans including reading, writing, speaking, and listening. Important for career opportunities in South Africa.

## CAPS Requirements
Structured progression: Grade 10 foundations (basic communication, simple texts), Grade 11 advanced concepts (complex texts, essay writing), Grade 12 integration and applications (advanced communication, literature). Assessment: 25% SBA (oral presentations, creative writing, comprehension tasks), 75% final examination (Paper 1: Language in Context, Paper 2: Literature, Paper 3: Creative Writing).

## Career Pathways
Afrikaans FAL is beneficial for:
- Teaching and Education (especially in Western Cape, Northern Cape)
- Government and Public Service
- Translation and Interpretation
- Media and Broadcasting
- Tourism and Hospitality (Western Cape region)
- Legal Services (bilingual advantage)
- Business and Commerce (Afrikaans-speaking markets)

## University Requirements
Required or beneficial for various programs:
- Teaching: Often required for teaching in Afrikaans-medium schools
- Humanities: Minimum Level 4 (50-59%) for most programs
- Some universities: Language requirement for certain programs
- Stellenbosch University: Beneficial for Afrikaans-medium programs

## APS Calculations
Afrikaans FAL contributes to APS calculations and can be used as one of the best 6 subjects. Particularly valuable for students planning to study or work in Afrikaans-speaking regions.

## Regional Advantages
Provides significant career advantages in Western Cape, Northern Cape, and Free State provinces. Essential for teaching positions in Afrikaans-medium schools and beneficial for government positions in these regions.
