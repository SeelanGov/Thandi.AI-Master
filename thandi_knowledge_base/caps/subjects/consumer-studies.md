## Overview
CAPS Consumer Studies covers food science, nutrition, textiles, housing, consumer economics, and entrepreneurship. Focuses on practical life skills, food preparation, and consumer awareness essential for daily living and related careers.

## CAPS Requirements
Structured progression: Grade 10 foundations (basic nutrition, textiles, consumer rights), Grade 11 advanced concepts (food science, housing, entrepreneurship), Grade 12 integration and applications (consumer economics, business planning). Assessment: 25% SBA (practical tasks, food preparation, projects), 75% final examination (Paper 1: Food and Nutrition, Paper 2: Clothing, Housing and Entrepreneurship).

## Career Pathways
Consumer Studies opens pathways to:
- Food science and technology careers
- Nutrition and dietetics (with additional science subjects)
- Hospitality and catering management
- Fashion design and textile industry
- Interior design and home economics
- Consumer protection and advocacy
- Food safety and quality control
- Entrepreneurship and small business development
- Retail management and merchandising
- Teaching (Consumer Studies and Life Skills)

## University Requirements
Universities accept Consumer Studies for relevant programs:
- Food Science: Minimum 50-60% (Level 4-5) plus Physical Sciences
- Nutrition: Minimum 55-65% (Level 4-5) plus Life Sciences
- Hospitality Management: Minimum 50-60% (Level 4-5)
- Fashion Design: Minimum 50-60% (Level 4-5)
- Consumer Science: Minimum 50-60% (Level 4-5)

## APS Calculations
Consumer Studies contributes fully to APS calculations. Particularly valuable for consumer science, hospitality, and design programs at universities and universities of technology.

## CAPS Vocational Advantage
CAPS Consumer Studies emphasizes practical skills, entrepreneurship, and South African consumer contexts - providing direct pathways to food industry, hospitality, and small business careers with immediate employment potential.
