## Overview
CAPS Marine Sciences covers oceanography, marine biology, coastal management, fisheries science, and marine conservation. Essential for South Africa's extensive coastline and marine economy careers.

## CAPS Requirements
Structured progression: Grade 10 foundations (marine ecosystems, coastal geography), Grade 11 advanced concepts (marine biology, oceanography, fisheries), Grade 12 integration and applications (marine conservation, coastal management, sustainable use). Assessment: 25% SBA (field studies, marine investigations, projects), 75% final examination (Paper 1: Marine Biology and Ecosystems, Paper 2: Oceanography and Marine Resources).

## Career Pathways
Marine Sciences opens pathways to:
- Marine biology and research careers
- Oceanography and marine geology
- Fisheries management and aquaculture
- Marine conservation and protected area management
- Coastal zone management and planning
- Marine tourism and eco-tourism
- Maritime law and policy development
- Marine engineering and technology
- Shipping and port management
- Marine environmental consulting

## University Requirements
Universities accept Marine Sciences for relevant programs:
- Marine Biology: Minimum 60-70% (Level 5-6) plus Life Sciences
- Oceanography: Minimum 60-70% (Level 5-6) plus Physical Sciences
- Environmental Science: Minimum 55-65% (Level 4-5)
- Geography: Minimum 50-60% (Level 4-5)
- Fisheries Science: Minimum 55-65% (Level 4-5)

## APS Calculations
Marine Sciences contributes fully to APS calculations. Particularly valuable for marine science programs at University of Cape Town, Rhodes University, and Nelson Mandela University.

## CAPS Vocational Advantage
CAPS Marine Sciences emphasizes South Africa's unique marine environment, practical coastal skills, and conservation awareness - providing pathways to marine careers in research, conservation, and the growing blue economy sector.
