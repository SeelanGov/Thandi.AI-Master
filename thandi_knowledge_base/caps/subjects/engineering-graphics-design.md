## Overview
CAPS Engineering Graphics and Design (EGD) covers technical drawing, computer-aided design (CAD), engineering problem-solving, and design communication. Essential for engineering, architecture, and technical design careers.

## CAPS Requirements
Structured progression: Grade 10 foundations (basic technical drawing, geometric construction), Grade 11 advanced concepts (orthographic projection, CAD software, design principles), Grade 12 integration and applications (engineering design process, 3D modeling, technical communication). Assessment: 25% SBA (practical drawing tasks, CAD projects, design portfolios), 75% final examination (Paper 1: Civil Technology and Mechanical Technology, Paper 2: Electrical Technology and Design Communication).

## Career Pathways
Engineering Graphics and Design opens pathways to:
- Mechanical and civil engineering
- Architecture and architectural technology
- Industrial design and product development
- Computer-aided design (CAD) specialist
- Technical drawing and drafting services
- Manufacturing and production engineering
- Quantity surveying and construction management
- Engineering consulting and project management
- 3D modeling and visualization
- Technical illustration and communication

## University Requirements
Universities accept EGD for relevant programs:
- Engineering: Minimum 60-70% (Level 5-6) plus Mathematics and Physical Sciences
- Architecture: Minimum 60-70% (Level 5-6) plus Mathematics
- Industrial Design: Minimum 55-65% (Level 4-5)
- Quantity Surveying: Minimum 55-65% (Level 4-5)
- Construction Management: Minimum 50-60% (Level 4-5)

## APS Calculations
Engineering Graphics and Design contributes fully to APS calculations. Particularly valuable for engineering, architecture, and design programs at all South African universities.

## CAPS Vocational Advantage
CAPS EGD emphasizes practical technical skills, CAD proficiency, and engineering problem-solving - providing direct pathways to technical careers with strong employment prospects in engineering, construction, and manufacturing industries.
