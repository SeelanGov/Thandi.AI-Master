## Overview
CAPS Economics covers microeconomics, macroeconomics, economic systems, and contemporary economic issues. Essential for understanding markets, policy, and economic decision-making.

## CAPS Requirements
Structured progression: Grade 10 foundations (basic economic concepts, markets, economic systems), Grade 11 advanced concepts (supply and demand, market failures, macroeconomic indicators), Grade 12 integration and applications (economic policy, international trade, development economics). Assessment: 25% SBA (tests, assignments, economic research projects), 75% final examination (data response and essay questions).

## Career Pathways
Economics is required or beneficial for:
- Economic Analysis and Research
- Banking and Financial Services
- Government Policy and Planning
- International Trade and Development
- Business Consulting and Strategy
- Investment Analysis and Portfolio Management
- Teaching (Economics and Business Studies)
- Journalism and Economic Commentary

## University Requirements
Required or beneficial for various programs:
- BCom Economics: Minimum 60-70% (Level 5-6)
- PPE (Politics, Philosophy, Economics): Minimum 65-70% (Level 5-6)
- International Relations: Minimum 60-65% (Level 5)
- Business Management: Often beneficial

## APS Calculations
Economics contributes to APS calculations for commerce, social science, and policy programs. Demonstrates analytical thinking and understanding of economic principles.

## Skills Development
Develops critical thinking, data analysis, policy evaluation, and economic reasoning skills. Excellent preparation for careers requiring analytical and strategic thinking about markets and economic systems.
