## Overview
CAPS Tourism covers tourism industry operations, destination marketing, travel services, hospitality management, and sustainable tourism development. Essential for South Africa's growing tourism sector and related service industries.

## CAPS Requirements
Structured progression: Grade 10 foundations (tourism basics, South African attractions), Grade 11 advanced concepts (tourism operations, marketing, customer service), Grade 12 integration and applications (tourism planning, sustainable development, entrepreneurship). Assessment: 25% SBA (practical projects, industry visits, presentations), 75% final examination (Paper 1: Tourism Industry and Operations, Paper 2: Tourism Development and Marketing).

## Career Pathways
Tourism opens pathways to:
- Travel agency and tour operator management
- Hotel and hospitality management
- Tourism marketing and destination promotion
- Event planning and conference management
- Adventure tourism and eco-tourism guiding
- Tourism development and planning
- Airline and transportation services
- Tourism research and consulting
- Cultural and heritage tourism management
- Tourism entrepreneurship and small business development

## University Requirements
Universities accept Tourism for relevant programs:
- Tourism Management: Minimum 50-60% (Level 4-5)
- Hospitality Management: Minimum 50-60% (Level 4-5)
- Event Management: Minimum 50-60% (Level 4-5)
- Marketing: Minimum 50-60% (Level 4-5)
- Business Studies: Minimum 50-60% (Level 4-5)

## APS Calculations
Tourism contributes fully to APS calculations. Particularly valuable for tourism, hospitality, and business programs at universities of technology and comprehensive universities.

## CAPS Vocational Advantage
CAPS Tourism emphasizes South African tourism assets, practical industry skills, and entrepreneurship - providing direct pathways to the growing tourism sector with excellent employment and business opportunities in South Africa's key economic sector.
