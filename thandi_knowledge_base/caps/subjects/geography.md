## Overview
CAPS Geography covers physical geography, human geography, and geographical skills including map work, spatial analysis, and environmental studies.

## CAPS Requirements
Structured progression: Grade 10 foundations (basic physical and human geography), Grade 11 advanced concepts (climate, population, development), Grade 12 integration and applications (environmental management, spatial planning). Assessment: 25% SBA (fieldwork, map work, research projects), 75% final examination (Paper 1: Climate and Weather, Geomorphology, Paper 2: Settlement, Economic Geography, Map Work).

## Career Pathways
Geography is beneficial for:
- Urban and Regional Planning
- Environmental Management and Conservation
- Geographic Information Systems (GIS)
- Surveying and Cartography
- Tourism and Hospitality Management
- Disaster Risk Management
- Teaching (Geography and Environmental Studies)
- Climate Change Research

## University Requirements
Useful for various programs:
- Environmental Science: Minimum 60-65% (Level 5)
- Urban Planning: Minimum 60-70% (Level 5-6)
- Tourism Management: Minimum 50-60% (Level 4-5)
- Geography degrees: Minimum 65-70% (Level 5-6)

## APS Calculations
Geography contributes to APS calculations for environmental, planning, and social science programs. Demonstrates spatial thinking and environmental awareness.

## Skills Development
Develops spatial analysis, environmental awareness, research skills, and understanding of human-environment interactions. Excellent preparation for careers in planning, environmental management, and sustainable development.
