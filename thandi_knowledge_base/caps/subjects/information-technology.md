## Overview
CAPS Information Technology covers programming, systems analysis, networking, and advanced computer science concepts. More technical and theoretical than Computer Applications Technology.

## CAPS Requirements
Structured progression: Grade 10 foundations (programming basics, computer systems), Grade 11 advanced concepts (advanced programming, networking, databases), Grade 12 integration and applications (systems development, project management). Assessment: 25% SBA (programming projects, systems analysis, portfolio), 75% final examination (programming, theory, and practical components).

## Career Pathways
Information Technology is essential for:
- Software Development and Programming
- Systems Analysis and Design
- Network Administration and Security
- Database Development and Administration
- Web Development and E-commerce
- Computer Science and Research
- Teaching (Information Technology and Computer Science)
- IT Project Management

## University Requirements
Required or highly beneficial for:
- Computer Science: Minimum 65-70% (Level 5-6)
- Information Technology: Minimum 60-70% (Level 5-6)
- Software Engineering: Minimum 65-75% (Level 5-6)
- Information Systems: Minimum 60-65% (Level 5)

## APS Calculations
Information Technology contributes significantly to APS calculations for computer science and IT programs. Demonstrates advanced technical skills and logical thinking.

## Technical Foundation
Provides strong foundation in programming languages, systems thinking, and technical problem-solving. Essential preparation for computer science degrees and software development careers.
