## Overview
CAPS Life Sciences covers biodiversity, ecology, evolution, genetics, human biology, and environmental studies. Essential for biological sciences, medicine, and environmental careers.

## CAPS Requirements
Structured progression: Grade 10 foundations (basic biology and ecology), Grade 11 advanced concepts (genetics, human systems), Grade 12 integration and applications (evolution, environmental issues). Assessment: 25% SBA (practical investigations, tests, assignments), 75% final examination (Paper 1: Biodiversity and Environment, Paper 2: Human Biology and Genetics).

## Career Pathways
Life Sciences is required for:
- Medicine, Dentistry, and Veterinary Science
- Biological sciences (Botany, Zoology, Microbiology)
- Environmental Science and Conservation
- Biotechnology and Biochemistry
- Pharmacy and Medical Technology
- Agriculture and Food Science
- Teaching (Life Sciences and Biology)
- Sports Science and Physiotherapy

## University Requirements
Most universities require Life Sciences for biological and medical programs:
- Medicine: Minimum 70-80% (Level 6-7)
- Biological Sciences: Minimum 60-70% (Level 5-6)
- Environmental Science: Minimum 60-65% (Level 5)
- Agriculture: Minimum 50-60% (Level 4-5)

## APS Calculations
Life Sciences contributes to APS calculations for biological and medical programs. Strong Life Sciences marks are essential for competitive programs like Medicine, Veterinary Science, and Biotechnology.

## Career Combinations
Often combined with Physical Sciences and Mathematics for medical careers, or with Geography for environmental careers. Can be paired with Agricultural Sciences for specialized agricultural programs.
