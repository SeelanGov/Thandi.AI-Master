## Overview
CAPS Agricultural Sciences covers crop production, animal husbandry, soil science, agricultural economics, and sustainable farming practices. Essential for agricultural careers, food security, and rural development in South Africa.

## CAPS Requirements
Structured progression: Grade 10 foundations (basic agriculture, plant/animal biology), Grade 11 advanced concepts (crop science, livestock management), Grade 12 integration and applications (agricultural economics, sustainability). Assessment: 25% SBA (practical investigations, farm visits, projects), 75% final examination (Paper 1: Crop and Pasture Science, Paper 2: Animal Studies and Agricultural Economics).

## Career Pathways
Agricultural Sciences opens pathways to:
- Commercial farming and agribusiness management
- Agricultural extension services and consulting
- Food production and processing industries
- Agricultural research and development
- Veterinary and animal health services
- Agricultural economics and policy development
- Sustainable agriculture and permaculture
- Agricultural technology and mechanization
- Seed and fertilizer industry careers
- Agricultural journalism and communication

## University Requirements
Universities accept Agricultural Sciences for relevant programs:
- Agricultural Sciences: Minimum 50-60% (Level 4-5)
- Veterinary Science: Minimum 65-75% (Level 5-6) plus other sciences
- Food Science: Minimum 55-65% (Level 4-5)
- Environmental Science: Minimum 50-60% (Level 4-5)
- Agricultural Economics: Minimum 50-60% (Level 4-5)

## APS Calculations
Agricultural Sciences contributes fully to APS calculations. Particularly valuable for agricultural programs at universities like University of Pretoria, Stellenbosch University, and University of KwaZulu-Natal.

## CAPS Vocational Advantage
CAPS Agricultural Sciences emphasizes practical farming skills, business management, and South African agricultural contexts - providing direct pathways to agricultural careers and rural economic development opportunities.
