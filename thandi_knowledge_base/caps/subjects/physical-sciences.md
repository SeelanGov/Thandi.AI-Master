## Overview
CAPS Physical Sciences combines Physics and Chemistry, covering mechanics, waves, electricity, matter and materials, chemical change, and chemical systems. Essential for engineering, medicine, and pure science careers.

## CAPS Requirements
Structured progression: Grade 10 foundations (basic physics and chemistry concepts), Grade 11 advanced concepts (organic chemistry, electricity, mechanics), Grade 12 integration and applications. Assessment: 25% SBA (practical investigations, tests, assignments), 75% final examination (Paper 1: Physics, Paper 2: Chemistry).

## Career Pathways
Physical Sciences is required for:
- All engineering disciplines (Mechanical, Electrical, Chemical, Civil)
- Medicine, Dentistry, and Veterinary Science
- Pure sciences (Physics, Chemistry, Biochemistry)
- Pharmacy and Medical Technology
- Geology and Mining Engineering
- Architecture and Quantity Surveying
- Teaching (Physical Sciences and related subjects)

## University Requirements
Most universities require Physical Sciences for STEM programs:
- Engineering: Minimum 60-70% (Level 5-6)
- Medicine: Minimum 70-80% (Level 6-7)
- Science degrees: Minimum 60-70% (Level 5-6)
- Pharmacy: Minimum 65-75% (Level 5-6)

## APS Calculations
Physical Sciences contributes significantly to APS calculations for STEM programs. Higher marks in Physical Sciences are crucial for competitive programs like Medicine and Engineering.

## Alternative Pathways
Students without Physical Sciences can access some science programs through bridging courses or alternative entry routes, but this significantly limits direct university admission options for STEM careers.
