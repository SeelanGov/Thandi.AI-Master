## Overview
Mathematical Literacy focuses on practical mathematics for everyday life, including financial literacy, data handling, measurement, and basic algebra. Designed for students who struggle with pure Mathematics.

## CAPS Requirements
Covers personal finance, data interpretation, measurement and geometry, probability, and algebraic thinking. Assessment: 25% SBA (assignments, tests, projects), 75% final exam (two papers: basic skills and applications).

## Career Limitations
Mathematical Literacy CANNOT be used for admission to:
- Engineering programs (requires Mathematics)
- Medicine and health sciences (requires Mathematics)
- Pure sciences (Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics degrees)
- Actuarial Science and Statistics
- Computer Science and IT degrees
- Architecture and quantity surveying

## Career Options Available
Mathematical Literacy IS accepted for:
- Business and management studies
- Education (except Mathematics/Science teaching)
- Social sciences and humanities
- Arts and creative fields
- Tourism and hospitality
- Marketing and communications
- Human resources
- Public administration
- Law (at some universities)

## University Requirements
Most universities require Mathematics (not Mathematical Literacy) for STEM fields. Students with Mathematical Literacy should focus on commerce, humanities, or social science programs. Some universities offer bridging courses to upgrade from Mathematical Literacy to Mathematics.

## Upgrade Options
Students can write Mathematics as a second additional language or take bridging courses at universities or FET colleges to access STEM programs later.
