## Overview
CAPS Accounting covers financial accounting, management accounting, auditing principles, and business ethics. Essential for commerce, business, and financial careers.

## CAPS Requirements
Structured progression: Grade 10 foundations (basic accounting principles, financial statements), Grade 11 advanced concepts (partnerships, companies, cost accounting), Grade 12 integration and applications (advanced financial statements, budgeting, internal control). Assessment: 25% SBA (tests, assignments, case studies), 75% final examination (comprehensive accounting problems and theory).

## Career Pathways
Accounting is required or beneficial for:
- Chartered Accountancy (CA(SA))
- Financial Management and Analysis
- Auditing and Assurance Services
- Management Accounting and Consulting
- Banking and Investment Services
- Business Management and Entrepreneurship
- Teaching (Accounting and Business Studies)
- Government Financial Management

## University Requirements
Most universities require Accounting for commerce programs:
- BCom Accounting: Minimum 60-70% (Level 5-6)
- BCom Financial Sciences: Minimum 60-65% (Level 5)
- Business Management: Minimum 50-60% (Level 4-5)
- Economics: Often beneficial but not always required

## APS Calculations
Accounting contributes significantly to APS calculations for commerce programs. Strong Accounting marks demonstrate analytical and numerical skills valued by business faculties.

## Professional Pathways
Strong foundation for professional qualifications like CA(SA), CIMA, ACCA, and CFA. Essential for students planning careers in financial services, auditing, or business management.
