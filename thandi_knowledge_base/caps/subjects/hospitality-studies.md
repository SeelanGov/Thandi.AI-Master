## Overview
CAPS Hospitality Studies covers hotel operations, food service management, customer relations, hospitality marketing, and industry entrepreneurship. Essential for South Africa's hospitality sector and service industry careers.

## CAPS Requirements
Structured progression: Grade 10 foundations (hospitality basics, customer service, food safety), Grade 11 advanced concepts (hotel operations, food service, marketing), Grade 12 integration and applications (hospitality management, entrepreneurship, industry trends). Assessment: 25% SBA (practical tasks, industry simulations, projects), 75% final examination (Paper 1: Hospitality Operations and Management, Paper 2: Food Service and Customer Relations).

## Career Pathways
Hospitality Studies opens pathways to:
- Hotel and resort management
- Restaurant and food service management
- Catering and banquet management
- Guest relations and concierge services
- Hospitality marketing and sales
- Event planning and venue management
- Cruise ship and travel hospitality
- Hospitality entrepreneurship and franchising
- Food and beverage operations
- Hospitality training and human resources

## University Requirements
Universities accept Hospitality Studies for relevant programs:
- Hospitality Management: Minimum 50-60% (Level 4-5)
- Hotel Management: Minimum 50-60% (Level 4-5)
- Food Service Management: Minimum 50-60% (Level 4-5)
- Tourism Management: Minimum 50-60% (Level 4-5)
- Business Management: Minimum 50-60% (Level 4-5)

## APS Calculations
Hospitality Studies contributes fully to APS calculations. Particularly valuable for hospitality, tourism, and business programs at universities of technology and comprehensive universities.

## CAPS Vocational Advantage
CAPS Hospitality Studies emphasizes practical industry skills, South African hospitality contexts, and entrepreneurship - providing direct pathways to the hospitality sector with excellent employment opportunities in hotels, restaurants, and tourism businesses.
