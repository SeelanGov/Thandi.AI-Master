## Overview
CAPS isiXhosa Home Language develops advanced proficiency in South Africa's second most widely spoken indigenous language, covering literature, creative writing, language structures, and cultural communication. Essential for careers requiring isiXhosa proficiency and cultural competency.

## CAPS Requirements
Structured progression: Grade 10 foundations (language structures, basic literature), Grade 11 advanced concepts (poetry, prose, drama, advanced grammar), Grade 12 integration and applications (literary analysis, creative writing, oral communication). Assessment: 25% SBA (oral presentations, creative writing, research projects), 75% final examination (Paper 1: Language Structures and Conventions, Paper 2: Literature, Paper 3: Writing and Presenting).

## Career Pathways
isiXhosa Home Language opens pathways to:
- Teaching (isiXhosa and African languages)
- Translation and interpretation services
- Journalism and media (isiXhosa publications, radio, TV)
- Publishing and editing (isiXhosa literature)
- Cultural heritage and museum work
- Government and public service (Eastern Cape, Western Cape focus)
- Legal services (court interpretation, community law)
- Social work and community development
- Tourism and cultural guiding
- Arts and creative industries (writing, theater, film)

## University Requirements
Universities accept isiXhosa Home Language for relevant programs:
- African Languages: Minimum 50-60% (Level 4-5)
- Education (Language Teaching): Minimum 50-60% (Level 4-5)
- Linguistics: Minimum 55-65% (Level 4-5)
- Social Sciences: Minimum 50-60% (Level 4-5)
- Journalism: Minimum 55-65% (Level 4-5)

## APS Calculations
isiXhosa Home Language contributes fully to APS calculations. Particularly valuable for language, education, and social science programs. Provides cultural competency advantage for careers in Eastern Cape, Western Cape, and Gauteng.

## CAPS Cultural Advantage
CAPS isiXhosa Home Language emphasizes cultural heritage, oral traditions, and contemporary isiXhosa literature - providing pathways to careers serving isiXhosa-speaking communities and preserving indigenous knowledge systems.
