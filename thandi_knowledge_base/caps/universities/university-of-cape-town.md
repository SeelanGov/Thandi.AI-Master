## Overview
UCT recognizes CAPS NSC using APS (Admission Point Score) calculations for most programs, with additional NBT requirements for many faculties.

## CAPS Admission Requirements
APS calculation: Best 6 subjects including English and Mathematics (where required)
Minimum endorsement: Bachelor's degree pass
NBT requirements: Academic Literacy and Quantitative Literacy tests required for most programs
Language requirements: English Home Language or First Additional Language minimum Level 4

## Faculty Requirements

### Engineering and Built Environment
Minimum APS: 42-45 points
Mathematics: Level 6-7 (70-100%)
Physical Sciences: Level 6 (70-79%)
English: Level 4-5 (50-69%)
NBT: Academic Literacy and Quantitative Literacy required

### Commerce and Management
Minimum APS: 40-45 points
Mathematics: Level 5-7 (60-100%) depending on program
English: Level 4-7 (50-100%) depending on program
NBT: Academic Literacy and Quantitative Literacy required

### Health Sciences (Medicine)
Minimum APS: 45+ points
Mathematics: Level 6-7 (70-100%)
Physical Sciences: Level 6-7 (70-100%)
Life Sciences: Level 6-7 (70-100%)
English: Level 5-6 (60-79%)
NBT: All three tests required

### Humanities and Social Sciences
Minimum APS: 38-42 points
English: Level 4-5 (50-69%)
Mathematics: Level 4+ if required by specific program
NBT: Academic Literacy required

## Program Examples
BSc Computer Science: APS 42+, Math Level 6, Physical Sciences Level 5, English Level 4
BCom Accounting: APS 42+, Math Level 6, English Level 5
BA Psychology: APS 40+, English Level 5, Math Level 4
MBChB Medicine: APS 45+, Math Level 6, Physical Sciences Level 6, Life Sciences Level 6

## CAPS vs IEB Differences
CAPS students use standard APS calculations (no FPS system)
No Advanced Programme subjects to exclude
NBT scores may be weighted differently for CAPS vs IEB students
Some programs may have different APS requirements for CAPS students
