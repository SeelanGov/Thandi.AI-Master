# South African Academic Calendar System 2025-2026: Comprehensive Research Report

**Research Date**: January 2026  
**Source**: Official Department of Basic Education (DBE), Government of South Africa  
**Compliance**: Based on National Education Policy Act and CAPS Curriculum Framework

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This comprehensive research document provides authoritative information about the South African academic calendar system for 2025-2026, including official term dates, examination schedules, assessment structures, and grade progression requirements. This information is essential for building a compliant academic calendar system that schools and students can trust.

**Key Finding**: As of 2026, South Africa has **unified the school calendar** - all provinces now follow the same start and end dates, eliminating the previous staggered calendar system.

## 3. GRADE-SPECIFIC ACADEMIC PROGRESSION REQUIREMENTS

### 3.1 Grade 10 (First Year of FET Phase)

**Academic Status**: Entry to Further Education and Training (FET) Phase  
**Subject Selection Timing**: Made in Grade 9 (previous year)  
**Marks Available**: No marks yet (new students)

#### Compulsory Subjects (Grade 10-12):
1. **Home Language** (one of 11 official SA languages)
2. **First Additional Language** (one of 11 official SA languages)
3. **Mathematics OR Mathematical Literacy** (choose one)
4. **Life Orientation** (not examined at NSC level)

#### Elective Subjects (Choose 3):
Students select 3 additional subjects from:
- Physical Sciences
- Biological Sciences
- History
- Geography
- Economics
- Business Studies
- Accounting
- Computer Applications Technology
- Information Technology
- Visual Arts
- Music
- Dance Studies
- Drama
- And others

**Total Subjects**: 7 subjects (6 examined + Life Orientation)

#### Subject Selection Constraints:
- **Mathematics Requirement**: Physical Sciences requires core Mathematics (not Mathematical Literacy)
- **Subject Combinations**: Must be viable (minimum 15 learners per combination)
- **Progression**: Subject choices made in Grade 9 carry through to Grade 12

---

### 3.2 Grade 11 (Second Year of FET Phase)

**Academic Status**: Continuing student with Grade 10 marks  
**Marks Available**: Grade 10 marks from previous year  
**Subject Changes**: Limited - most students continue with Grade 10 subjects

#### Key Progression Points:
- **Mark Improvement**: Focus on improving Grade 10 marks
- **University Planning**: Begin researching university requirements
- **Subject Viability**: Subject combinations must remain viable
- **Mathematical Literacy Switch**: If switching from Mathematics to Mathematical Literacy (or vice versa), must drop Physical Sciences and select new subjects

#### Assessment Structure:
- Continuous Assessment (CASS): Throughout the year
- Term marks: Recorded each term
- Progression to Grade 12: Based on Grade 11 performance

---

### 3.3 Grade 12 (Final Year - Matric)

**Academic Status**: Final year of secondary education  
**Marks Available**: Grade 11 marks from previous year  
**Critical Period**: Determines university admission and career pathways

#### NSC Assessment Structure:
- **Continuous Assessment (CASS)**: 40% of final mark
  - Tests, assignments, projects, practical tasks
  - Oral presentations
  - Portfolio work
  - Spread throughout the year
  
- **Final Examination**: 60% of final mark
  - Written examinations (October-November)
  - Practical assessments (where applicable)
  - Rewrite opportunities (May-June)

#### Subject Requirements:
- **Minimum 6 subjects** (Life Orientation not counted)
- **Compulsory subjects**: Home Language, First Additional Language, Mathematics/Mathematical Literacy, Life Orientation
- **Elective subjects**: 3 additional subjects

#### Promotion Requirements:
- Must achieve minimum marks in subjects
- Progression to university depends on:
  - **APS (Admission Points Score)**: Calculated from best 6 subjects
  - **Subject-specific requirements**: Some programs require specific subjects
  - **Minimum marks**: Varies by university and program

## 5. PROVINCIAL VARIATIONS AND CALENDAR CHANGES

### 5.1 2026 Calendar Unification (Major Change)

**Previous System (2025 and earlier)**:
- **Staggered Calendar**: Inland vs. Coastal provinces
- **Inland Schools** (started earlier): Gauteng, Free State, Mpumalanga, Limpopo, North West
- **Coastal Schools** (started later): KwaZulu-Natal, Western Cape, Eastern Cape, Northern Cape

**New System (2026 onwards)**:
- **Unified Calendar**: All provinces start and end on same dates
- **Effective from**: 2026 academic year
- **Rationale**: Standardization, improved coordination, national consistency

### 5.2 Provincial Education Departments

While calendar is now unified, provincial departments may have minor variations in:
- **School holiday dates**: Some provinces may have additional provincial holidays
- **Assessment schedules**: Timing of provincial assessments
- **Preliminary exam dates**: May vary slightly by province

## 6. SUBJECT SELECTION AND ACADEMIC PROGRESSION TIMING

### 6.1 Subject Selection Timeline

**Grade 9** (Year before FET Phase):
- **Term 1-2**: Career guidance and subject exploration
- **Term 3**: Subject selection process begins
- **Term 4**: Final subject selections submitted
- **Deadline**: Usually by end of October/early November

**Grade 10-12**: 
- Subject combinations locked in
- Limited changes allowed (only in exceptional circumstances)

### 6.2 Subject Selection Constraints

#### Mathematics vs. Mathematical Literacy
- **Core Mathematics**: Required for Physical Sciences, Engineering, Science programs
- **Mathematical Literacy**: Suitable for Commerce, Humanities, Social Sciences
- **Switch Penalty**: Changing from Mathematics to Mathematical Literacy in Grade 11 forces dropping Physical Sciences

#### Subject Viability
- **Minimum Enrollment**: Each subject combination needs minimum 15 learners
- **Timetable Clashes**: Subject combinations must not clash on timetable
- **Resource Constraints**: Some schools may not offer all subjects

#### Language Requirements
- **Home Language**: Must be one of 11 official SA languages
- **First Additional Language**: Must be different from Home Language
- **Bilingual Advantage**: Students can take both languages at higher level

## 8. ASSESSMENT STRUCTURE AND WEIGHTING

### 8.1 NSC Assessment Framework

**Continuous Assessment (CASS)**: 40% of final mark
- **Purpose**: Monitor ongoing learning and progress
- **Components**:
  - Class tests and quizzes
  - Assignments and projects
  - Practical tasks and experiments
  - Oral presentations
  - Portfolio work
  - Practical examinations (for practical subjects)
- **Timing**: Throughout the academic year
- **Recording**: Marks recorded and submitted to examination boards

**Final Examination**: 60% of final mark
- **Purpose**: Summative assessment of year's learning
- **Timing**: October-November (Grade 12)
- **Duration**: Approximately 5 weeks
- **Format**: Written examinations, practical assessments
- **Rewrite Opportunity**: May-June of following year

### 8.2 Subject-Specific Assessment

**Practical Subjects** (Physical Sciences, Biological Sciences, etc.):
- **Practical Assessment**: 25% of final mark
- **Theory Examination**: 75% of final mark
- **Practical Exams**: Conducted during examination period

**Language Subjects**:
- **Listening/Oral**: Component of continuous assessment
- **Reading and Writing**: Examined in final papers
- **Comprehension**: Tested throughout

**Mathematics**:
- **Problem-solving**: Emphasized in both CASS and exams
- **Calculation**: Core component
- **Application**: Real-world problem scenarios

## 9. UNIVERSITY ADMISSION AND APS CALCULATION

### 9.1 Admission Points Score (APS)

**Calculation**: Based on best 6 subjects (excluding Life Orientation)  
**Points Scale**: 
- 7 (80-100%): 7 points
- 6 (70-79%): 6 points
- 5 (60-69%): 5 points
- 4 (50-59%): 4 points
- 3 (40-49%): 3 points
- 2 (30-39%): 2 points
- 1 (0-29%): 1 point

**Maximum APS**: 42 points (7 × 6 subjects)  
**Typical University Requirements**: 24-36 APS (varies by program)

### 9.2 Subject-Specific Requirements

**Engineering Programs**:
- Require: Core Mathematics + Physical Sciences
- Typical APS: 30-36

**Commerce/Business Programs**:
- Require: Mathematics or Mathematical Literacy + Accounting/Economics
- Typical APS: 24-30

**Science Programs**:
- Require: Physical Sciences + Biological Sciences + Mathematics
- Typical APS: 28-36

**Humanities/Social Sciences**:
- Flexible subject requirements
- Typical APS: 20-28
